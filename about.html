About Me

I'm Karel Durovic, also known online as Lordury — an IT technician, automation enthusiast, AI coach, and occasional 3D printing tinkerer.

Personal Story

I live in Jinočany and work in Holešovice, Prague, currently at Accenture as an IT technician. My path has always been about making things work a bit smoother — whether it’s computers, workflows, or everyday routines. I like practical solutions, clear explanations, and tools that actually help people instead of impressing them with buzzwords.

Outside of work, life is happily busy: my spouse is on maternity leave, and we have two daughters — one starting school and one newborn. So yes, there’s a lot of noise, a lot of love, and not a lot of sleep.

Work & Focus

Professionally, I’m an IT technician working mostly in Windows environments. I focus on troubleshooting, support, and optimizing workflows so that people spend less time fighting their tools and more time doing actual work.

Over time, I’ve shifted more and more towards automation and scripting:

  • BAT scripting and PowerShell for everyday IT tasks
  • Python for automation and small utilities (including PyAutoGUI)
  • Power Automate and Power Apps for building practical, low‑cost solutions

My main philosophy is simple: automation should be accessible. I try to focus on solutions that can be achieved for free or at a budget‑friendly price, so that individuals and smaller teams can benefit without needing enterprise budgets.

AI Coaching

I also work as an AI coach, helping people understand and use AI tools in a practical way. My main topics are:

  • Microsoft Copilot 365 in everyday work
  • Agentic AI workflows and how to think in terms of tasks and agents
  • Introductory AI concepts for people who are curious but not technical

I prefer a down‑to‑earth approach: no hype, no fear, just clear examples and realistic expectations. The goal is to make AI feel like a helpful colleague, not a mysterious black box.

Interests

When I’m not fixing something or automating it, I enjoy:

  • Strategy games and MOBA games — thinking, planning, reacting
  • 3D modeling and 3D printing experiments, including both useful prints and glorious failures
  • Running several YouTube channels and experimenting with content
  • History, political and demographical issues and public behavior

Most of my interests revolve around systems — whether it’s a game, a workflow, a city, or a machine. I like understanding how things fit together and how small changes can improve the whole. Small details can make a huge difference.

Values & Convictions

My main moral foundation comes from Christianity. It shapes how I think about responsibility, honesty, and how we treat other people. I don’t try to push this on anyone, but it quietly guides how I work, how I communicate, and how I make decisions.

In practice, this means I try to stay humble, avoid pretending I know everything, and focus on being genuinely helpful. Whether it’s through automation, AI coaching, or a simple script that saves someone a few minutes every day — it all counts.
